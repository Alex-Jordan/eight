We then form the missing sequence \(x\) by assigning at each position the opposite value to the one at the corresponding position of the diagonal sequence. Thus, the sequence \(x\) begins G, G, \(8\text{,}\), \(8\text{,}\), G, ... . This Great \(8\) sequence will be different from every sequence in the list. In particular, the missing sequence \(x\) will be different from the \(n\)th sequence in the list because their \(n\)th terms will differ. We are forced to conclude that no pairing of \(\mathbb{N}\) and \(\mathbb{S}\) is possible.
