Cantor argued that this couldn't possibly be a complete pairing of the two sets by demonstrating that there must be some element in \(\mathbb{S}\) that is not in this list. The missing sequence, let's call it \(x\text{,}\), is constructed by first considering the “diagonal” entries in this listing, as boxed. These diagonal entries themselves determine a Great \(8\) sequence (which begins \(8\text{,}\), \(8\text{,}\), G, G, \(8, \ldots\)).
